# 📐 SyntheticInfrageometry

This repository contains code for studying what geometric constructions an observer constrained to a discrete substrate, the so-called **infra-observer**, would develop. We focus here on discrete substrates given by graphs or hypergraphs produced by Wolfram hypergraph rewriting.

Classical geometry grew out of the measurement practices of a macro-observer, such as using a [knotted rope](https://p135246.github.io/math/2021/08/25/field-tax-in-old-egypt.html), which implements the concepts of straightedge, compass, and unit. Using the tools available to an infra-observer, who can seemingly only walk on the graph, we try to develop similar geometric concepts and follow the same conceptual progression that led from geometric practice to **Euclidean axioms** and later to analytic and algebraic geometry. This approach is grounded in the assumption that our continuous abstraction of the macro-world corresponds to a limit of an expanding discrete system at macroscopic scale, as in the **computational-universe model** of the [Wolfram Physics Project](https://www.wolframphysics.org/) generated by hypergraph rewriting.

Constructions in the discrete world are notoriously **ambiguous**, so the same infra-geometric construction may branch at every step, producing several valid final infrageometric scenes. This leads to novel questions about comparing the scenes, measuring their distance, and studying the stability of constructions. The geometric scene one should have in mind is that of a **diffuse picture** where each vertex and edge of the substrate shaded by how many candidates pass through it.

The capabilities of an infra-observer are determined by what the substrate lets him see, so the project organises substrates into a four-rung **labelling hierarchy**. On an **unlabeled graph** he cannot tell vertices apart and only records the sequence of degrees along his walk; his observational data are the empirical statistics of degree sequences sampled over many walks. On a **ribbon graph** he additionally remembers which edge he came in along, so he can run a breadth- or depth-first sweep up to a fixed depth around himself, but he still cannot decide whether a freshly visited vertex is new or one already seen via another edge. On a **vertex-labeled graph** every vertex has a distinct identity and he can enumerate *paths* in the graph-theoretic sense — though parallel edges between the same pair of vertices remain indistinguishable to him. Only on a **vertex- and edge-labeled graph** is every step fully resolved and he enumerates *walks* in the most general sense.

This labelling hierarchy aligns with **physical scales**. The unlabeled graph is the *quantum scale*: no Euclidean geometry exists, only random-walk estimates of dimension and curvature. The vertex-labeled graph is the *mesoscale*, where ambiguous Euclidean constructions become possible and produce the diffuse scenes described above. The *macroscale* is the limit in which — for some constructions, and presumably not for others — diffusion sharpens into delta functions and classical Euclidean uniqueness emerges.

**Our goals include:**

- defining infra-geometric primitives and postulates
- studying **walks on graphs** along the labelling hierarchy (unlabeled → ribbon → vertex-labeled → fully labeled): which properties of the substrate — degree distributions, assortativity, spectral and Hausdorff dimensions, projected curvature, girth, faces, genus, full Euclidean structure — become accessible at each rung, and where each observer hits its information ceiling
- building a framework for branching constructions
- introducing various types of coordinates, and the analytization and algebraization of geometry
- developing new exotic concepts and connections, such as to tropical geometry
- performing a ruliological study of which postulates hold on which graphs
- performing an empirical study of whether ambiguous infra-geometric constructions converge to a unique construction at macroscopic scale as the substrate is refined
- incorporating hypergraphs and n-ary relations, or developing a new exotic infra-geometry for them
