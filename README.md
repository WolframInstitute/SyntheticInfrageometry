# 📐 SyntheticInfrageometry

We study geometric constructions that an observer living on a graph or hypergraph would develop. Classical geometry grew out of the measurement practices of a macro-observer, based on the use of straightedge, compass, and units, implemented for instance by a [knotted rope](https://p135246.github.io/math/2021/08/25/field-tax-in-old-egypt.html). We ask what analogous notions an infra-observer on a discrete substrate would develop, and how such an observer would abstract these constructions. We try to follow the same kind of conceptual progression that led from geometric praxis to Euclidean axioms and their various versions, and then to analytic and algebraic geometry. Our motivation is the idea that our seemingly continuous macro-world emerges as a limit of a discrete system as complexity increases, in particular through hypergraph rewriting as in the [Wolfram Physics Project](https://www.wolframphysics.org/).

A characteristic property of constructions in the discrete world is ambiguity. In synthetic infrageometry, the same recipe for geometric construction may branch at every step and produce several equally valid infrageometric scenes. This leads naturally to questions of confluence, meaningful ways to measure the distance between geometric scenes, and the stability of constructions.

**Our goals include:**

- developing sensible definitions of infra-geometric primitives and postulates
- studying "ruliologically" which postulates hold on which graphs
- developing a framework for dealing with branching constructions
- exploring analytization and algebraization of the geometry through various types of coordinates and associated algebraic operations
- exploring connections to other geometries such as tropical geometry
- developing experiments to study whether infra-geometric constructions converge to classical continuous constructions as the substrate is refined
- exploring the role of hypergraphs and n-ary relations in this geometry
